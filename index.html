  Facilitating International Investments & Partnerships


  • Begin

Topl bridges the boundaries that keep capital locked up

The idea behind Topl is simple: build the financial infrastructure necessary to bring low-cost capital to developing economies. The barriers that hinder investment into developing economies not only deprive much many regions of the capital necessary for an economy to prosper, but also block investors from accessing premier opportunities. To break down these barriers and enable low-friction investments anywhere in the world, Topl introduces a public blockchain to source and track these investments and their outcomes.

Core Components

Topl’s platform was designed with three defining and unique pillars:

Our Investment Model
Open-ended equity investments are often too risky in developing markets with inadequate liquidity, and debt can quickly stifle innovation and expansion when interest rates begin to exceed 15%. This is why Topl’s investment platform connects companies and investors through short to medium term joint ventures. We seek to align the interests of companies and investors more effectively than traditional equity or debt investments. To future reduce friction and maximize returns, contracts aren’t settled in cash, but rather through the commodity asset produced.
Commodity-Backed Stable Currency
Currency exchange represents one of the major barriers to the global flow of capital, especially when currency pegs or volatility are involved. Instead of national fiat, Topl’s platform uses a price-stable cryptocurrency, the Poly, for all transactions. The Poly is ideal for conducting international investments because it’s is backed by a basket of universally valuable commodities, not by any one national bank or government.
Decentralized Reputation System
When operating across multiple business and regulatory domains, trust is a major issue between potential partners. While credit scores and financial records are good standards inside a single legal domain, they are often insufficient evidence in international transactions. To solve this problem, Topl has developed the first blockchain-based reputation system for businesses

Process

Let's go through a typical investment sequence for Topl.

  • Scroll

Request for Investment

A Commodity Producer seeking funds may use Topl’s blockchain platform to find a investment partner for a joint venture. The Producer publishes the requested amount along with initial terms, including the commodity to be produced, project location, and profit share, through the peer-to-peer blockchain network to be viewed by potential investors.

  • Continue →

Contract Negotiation

After an investment proposal has been submitted, potential Investors may view the proposal and summary information regarding the Producer. If an Investor believes they may be interested in financing the project, then they will submit their signature to the Producer to indicate their interest. The Producer can then decide to accept or reject the overture and negotiations may begin.

  • Continue →

Signing & Submission

Once the Producer and Investor have reached an agreement on the terms of their joint venture, only one additional step is required. To facilitate the disbursement of funds and handle the sale of the commodities produced, a third entity known as a Hub serves as an intermediary. A Hub, most often chosen for its physical proximity to the Producer, acts as an essential link between financial transactions on the blockchain and the movement of real goods.

  • Continue →

Fund Transfer & Disbursement

All transfers and account balances on the Topl blockchain are denoted in the platform’s native cryptocurrency, the Poly, which may be purchased either through an exchange or directly from a Hub. A signed and submitted contract proposal enters into force only with the transfer of the appropriate amount of Polys from the Investor into escrow administered by the contract. The contract in turn dispenses the funds from escrow to the Producer.

  • Continue →

Commodity Delivery

After receiving the funds, the Producer begins work on satisfying the contract. Along with a schedule for delivery, the contract may specify either one-time or periodic shipment of a commodity to the Hub. The use of the Hub in this function serves a dual purpose: to provide the Producer with a streamlined mechanism to offload commodities and to allow to the Investor to remain confident in the fulfillment of the contract in spite of often remote geographies.

  • Continue →

Contract Fulfillment & Payment

After the Producer finishes delivery of the commodity, which must be confirmed by the Hub, the contract is considered to be fulfilled. At this time, the Hub will dispense Polys to the Producer and Investor based on the profit share dictated in the contract. Finally, the state of the blockchain will reflect this fulfilled and paid contract, and the reputation scores of all involved parties will be updated to reflect the outcome of the contract.

Features

Topl is committed to providing a secure, accessible, and powerful method to invest anywhere in the world. Based on those ideals, we’ve focused on developing these key features.

Investment Smart Contract Templating

Investors and producers agree to contract terms that are enforced on the chain. These contracts automatically apply reputation modifiers and submit notifications to participants as deadlines approach and information becomes available.

Integrated Reputation Tracking

Producers, investors, and hubs earn a score based on agreement outcomes. This score signals credibility, reliability, and past involvement, allowing future investors to avoid fraud, helping effective hubs gain legitimacy, and giving competitive producers leverage for improved terms.

Fundamentally Stable Units

Because polys are backed by a basket of commodities, their value naturally tracks the commodity market as a whole, eliminating volatility from speculation on any of the individual commodities or on the poly itself. Because Polys are backed by the production flow through the Topl platform itself, there’s also no need for a central bank to dictate monetary policy.

Interoperable Tokens

Polys and Arbits will both be available as standard ERC20 tokens on Ethereum as well as other chains that allow the transfer of tokenized value. The ability to move tokens between these chains and Topl will be handled through off-chain cryptographic transactions, enabling liquid markets and robust access.

Granular Privacy Controls

Investment activities are naturally expected to be kept private. However, information needs to be accessible when potential investment arises. We enable anonymous investment agreements to coexist with the need for financial disclosure by combining an encrypted public bulletin with zkSNARK-enabled transactions.

Simple, Accessible Interface

Complexity can be intimidating. The Topl client is meant to simplify network participation for basic use by abstracting away cryptographic management while also allowing power users to easily interact with the system. A well-designed graphical user interface is key to this process.

Roadmap

This is intended to be a forward-looking perspective on the expected milestones for development of the Topl network. These are subject to minor changes as implementation moves forward.

Whitepaper Published

The first draft of the whitepaper is made publicly viewable and discusses the economic details and technical overview for the network.

June 2017

Alpha Client Release

First testnet client is released for early evaluation of networking, security, and functionality.

July 2017

Public Testnet Launch

The testnet client is made publicly available and bootstrapping nodes will be always on. Transfer, initial consensus protocol, and basic investment flow can be evaluated.

July 2017

Initial Yellowpaper Release

The first iteration of the yellowpaper is released with specific detail for the technical specifications of the network — both current and future.

July 2017

ICO Details Announcement

Information regarding the future sale of Arbits — such as schedule, preliminary terms, and process — is disseminated.

June 2017

Bug Bounty & Tester Rewards Begin

Network testers and those who discover network vulnerabilities or flaws begin to be rewarded with Arbits that will be distributed in the genesis block.

September 2017

Feature Freeze for Mainnet Launch

Development of additional functionality halts. Focus turns to further security testing, code review, and client platform support.

November 2017

Initial Arbit Sale

The sale of a portion of Arbits for the system is conducted to raise funds for the continued development and maintenance of the network.

December 2017

First Platform Investments Secured

First round of prospective businesses and capital are committed to a large-scale trial of the system.

February 2018

Mainnet Launch

The mainnet is launched for a full-scale trial of international investment. Arbits are distributed according to initial sale results and distribution allotment.

March 2018

Fulfillment of Initial Platform Investments

The initial set of platform investments begins to see fulfillment in delivery of commodities.

November 2018

First Independent Hub Operators

Hubs not directly associated with the developers of the network begin servicing capital contracts through the platform.
